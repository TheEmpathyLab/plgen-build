## What was decided

- **Organizational model: Wikimedia/Creative Commons over NPR.** Entirely donor/community funded, independence as brand. Governing line: "Funded by the people who use it, accountable to no one else." The co-op membership model ($4/mo, $40/yr, $99 founding) maps cleanly to this precedent.
- **PLGen stays creator-side, not platform-side.** The moment PLGen pitches itself to AI companies as a compliance layer for them, it becomes their tool. Strategic position: observable, creator-funded, accountable to no one else.
- **Creative Commons is a conversation worth having.** CC answers "What can you do with this work?" PLGen answers "How was this work made?" — no overlap, different layers. CC has navigated the institutional adoption road PLGen is about to travel. Possible org-to-org conversation when the time is right.
- **Academic road show is the confirmed next community-building move.** Universities are in crisis over AI disclosure with no shared vocabulary or tooling. Target professors before deans — professors make culture, deans make policy. Real faculty adoption generates real registry usage, which becomes institutional proof of concept.
- **SAT/ACT essay angle is worth pursuing as an anchor story.** A PLGen requirement on admissions essays reframes the label as a character signal, not a compliance mechanism. College Board and ACT Inc. are under pressure on this with no solution. PLGen could be what they point to.
- **Anti-sycophancy correction added to both prompts.** AI sycophancy in split estimation was identified as the highest-priority threat: structural, invisible, systematically skews every label in the same direction. Fix: explicit non-negotiable split estimation instruction. Neither the human nor the AI can persuade the model to reweight after calculation.
- **PLGen Confidence Layer is a confirmed feature.** Secondary trust signal displayed alongside every label — individual observable signals, not a calculated score (gameable, reductive). GH Issue filed this session.

## GitHub Issue filed this session

**[Feature] PLGen Confidence Layer — Label Weight Signal** (see issue for full spec)

Core concept: two-layer architecture
- **Layer 1 (persuadable):** Human/AI split percentage. Requires judgment. Human can adjust.
- **Layer 2 (non-persuadable):** Confidence signals. Derived from objective session metadata — timestamps, token counts, paste events, gap detection. Cannot be reweighted by user instruction or AI sycophancy.

Signal categories: Session Integrity (highest weight), Input Transparency, Identity and Membership, Output and Artifact, Corroboration.

Institutional threshold path: PLGen provides signals. Communities define what weight they require. Same model as Creative Commons — CC doesn't enforce licenses, courts and communities do.
